Course overview

What is Basic Life Support?

Basic Life Support is foundational emergency-response training centered on the first critical actions taken when someone experiences cardiac arrest, respiratory arrest, or another life-threatening emergency. The course connects early recognition with practical response skills so participants can act in an organized, confident way while additional help is being activated.

  • Recognize when a person needs immediate lifesaving assistance.
  • Practice high-quality adult, child, and infant CPR skills appropriate to the course.
  • Understand how an AED supports the response to cardiac arrest.
  • Coordinate compressions and ventilations with other responders.
  • Strengthen communication and role clarity during a simulated emergency.
