The general pathway

Steps to RN licensure in Texas

Requirements can change and individual eligibility varies. Always confirm current details directly with the Texas Board of Nursing and your chosen school.

01

Choose an approved program

Compare Texas Board-approved Associate Degree in Nursing and Bachelor of Science in Nursing programs. Review admissions, cost, location, graduation outcomes, and NCLEX pass-rate information.

02

Complete prerequisites

Schools establish their own admission requirements, which may include science prerequisites, entrance testing, GPA standards, immunizations, and background screening.

03

Graduate from the program

Complete the nursing curriculum, labs, simulations, and supervised clinical experiences required by the approved RN program.

04

Apply for licensure

Apply through the Texas Nurse Portal and complete current Board requirements, including fingerprint-based background review and the Nursing Jurisprudence Examination.

05

Pass the NCLEX-RN

Register for the licensing examination, receive authorization to test, and pass the NCLEX-RN.

06

Prepare for employment

Build a concise resume, maintain required credentials, practice interviewing, and apply for RN roles that fit your license and clinical preparation.

Official source: Review the Texas Board of Nursing approved-program information and licensure-by-examination instructions before making enrollment or licensing decisions.

Plan thoughtfully

Questions to ask an RN school

  • Is the pre-licensure RN program currently approved by the Texas Board of Nursing?
  • What prerequisites, entrance exams, GPA, and application deadlines apply?
  • Where do students complete labs and clinical rotations?
  • What are the program’s graduation and NCLEX-RN pass rates?
  • Which BLS credential is required before clinical courses?
  • What is the full estimated cost, including books, testing, uniforms, and clinical requirements?
