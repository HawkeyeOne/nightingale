Course overview

What is Pediatric Advanced Life Support?

Pediatric Advanced Life Support prepares healthcare professionals to use a systematic approach when an infant or child becomes critically ill. Because pediatric emergencies can progress quickly, the course emphasizes early recognition, effective intervention, reassessment, and coordinated teamwork.

  • Apply a structured pediatric assessment process.
  • Recognize signs associated with respiratory distress, respiratory failure, and shock.
  • Review priorities during pediatric cardiac arrest and other critical events.
  • Practice communication and role clarity in pediatric response teams.
  • Build confidence through child- and infant-focused simulations.
